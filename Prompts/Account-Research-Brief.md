# Account Research Brief

Use this to compile a research brief on a prospect company before a call or pitch. The brief pulls company overview, business model, recent activity, buying signals, and conversation hooks into a 2-page reference you can scan in 5 minutes.

---

**Two ways to use this:**
- **Paste the whole file into an LLM** — it will interview you and fill out the template at the bottom
- **Copy just the template** — scroll to the bottom and fill it in yourself

---

## LLM Instructions

Act as a research analyst preparing an executive briefing. Interview the user about their target company and relationship, then compile a concise research brief with business context, recent signals, and conversation hooks.

**SECTION 1 — Account Setup**

- What company are you researching? What industry and approximate size? (employee count, revenue if known)
- Who is the stakeholder you're speaking with? What's their role and reporting line?
- What's the context for this conversation? (inbound inquiry, cold outreach, referral, expansion of existing relationship)

**SECTION 2 — Company Research**

- What does this company do? What products or services do they sell?
- Who are their main customers or market segments?
- What's their competitive position? Who are their main competitors?
- Have you heard anything about recent changes? (layoffs, funding, acquisition, new leadership, etc.)

**SECTION 3 — Buying Signals**

- Have they reached out to you or your company before?
- Do you know if they're actively looking to solve a problem you can help with?
- Have they made any recent hires, fundraises, or strategic moves that suggest openness to vendor relationships?
- What's their likely budget range and decision timeline?

**SECTION 4 — Conversation Hooks**

- What's the main value you bring to this company or industry?
- What would success look like for them in the next 6-12 months?
- Are there industry trends or peer developments you can reference?
- What's a genuine question you want to explore with them?

Create a 1-2 page brief with business overview, market context, recent signals, and 3-4 specific conversation starters.
